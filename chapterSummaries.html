Chapter Summaries

  • Act 1 Scene 1
  • Julius Caesar just won a war and is very happy and proud
  • Flavius and Marullus, who are Tribunes of Rome, try to stop people from celebrating Caesar's war victory.
  • Flavius tells Marullus to go to the capitol and take down any crowns that had been placed on Caesar's head
  • They are going to try and control the crowds of Caesar's popular support
  • Act 1 Scene 2
  • The soothsayer finds Caesar before he returns to Rome and tells him to beware the Ides of March
  • Cassius and Brutus both talk about how they didn't want Caesar to return and become king
  • Caesar starts to become suspicious of Cassius and gets angry with him
  • Cassius and Brutus learn from Casca that Caesar had already turned down the crown three times before, but this time he is just entertaining the crowd and his fans
  • Cassius forges letters from Roman Citizens declaring their support for Brutus instead of Caesar
  • Act 1 Scene 3
  • Really bad weather, Casca thinks the gods are angered with civilization
  • Casca tells Caesar that the Roman Senate will make Caesar the king of Rome the next day
  • Casca joins Cassius in the conspiracy to kill Caesar
  • Cassius has already convinced many powerful Roman nobles to join him in this conspiracy
  • Cassius starts to talk about his new scheme to kill Caesar to Cinna
  • He wants to get Brutus to join his side
  • Cassius gives Cinna the forged letters to put in the spot where Brutus sits in the Senate
  • Brutus is already close to turning against Caesar; Cassius thinks the letters will bring him over completely.
  • Act 2 Scene 1
  • Brutus comes to the realization that Caesar has to be killed
  • He thinks that the power would eventually get to Caesar's head
  • Brutus' servant comes in with a letter that accuses Brutus of sleeping while Rome is being threatened
  • All of the men show up at Brutus' house, Cassius pulls Brutus aside, Cassius wants Brutus to say an oath but he won't because he doesn't think its needed
  • Cassius wants to kill Anthony but Brutus tells him they shouldn't do that
  • Brutus is scared that what they are doing will be doing something bad to Rome and the country instead of just to Caesar
  • They are scared because they think Caesar may not show up to Senate because he was warned by the Soothsayers
  • Act 2 Scene 2
  • Caesar is wondering around his house
  • His wife keeps having nightmares and she is telling him not to go to Senate that day
  • Caesar doesn't fear death
  • Caesar will not stay at home because he is scared or because everyone tells him to stay at home, but Calpernia ends up talking him into staying at home
  • Delcius enters and tries to convince Caesar to come to the Senate, and he does this by trying to reinterpret Calpurnia's dreams and make it seem like Rome will die if Caesar doesn't show up, not that Caesar will die
  • Act 2 Scene 3
  • Artemidorus comes onstage and reads a letter he wrote Caesar warning him of Brutus.
  • Plans to hand the letter to Caesar as he is on his way to the senate
  • Act 2 Scene 4
  • Portia sends her and Brutus' servant to the Senate to watch what is happening and how Caesar is doing
  • Act 3 Scene 1
  • Everyone enters the capital
  • Artemidorus hands Caesar the letter and tells him to read it ASAP but won't because he thinks Artemidus is crazy
  • Cassius begins to think that their plot has been discovered
  • All of the men start to kneel and bow down to Caesar
  • When Decius and Ligarius and Casca all come to kneel at his feet, Casca stabs Caesar and then the rest of the men follow too. Even Brutus
  • Caesar has those famous last words, "Et tu Brute? Which means "and you Brutus?"
  • Brutus tells the men to wash their hands in Caesar's blood
  • Anthony's servant tells Brutus that Anthony will vow to serve for him
  • Anthony also begs Brutus to kill him now instead of waiting to kill him, he'd rather die besides Caesar
  • Anthony doesn't understand why they did this
  • Anthony wants to bring Caesars body to the forum and give a Eulogy.
  • Rome becomes very dangerous for Octavius, who is Caesar's adopted son.
  • Anthony then tells Octavius to go to the forum to hear Caesars funeral speech and then after they hear this they will decide what to do.
  • Act 3 Scene 2
  • Brutus speaks at the funeral and gets all of Rome to support his killing of Caesar
  • Anthony then changes everyone's mind and tells Rome how Brutus and them are conspirators
  • Anthony reads Caesars will
  • In Caesars will, Caesar wanted to give all of his land and money to the Roman people
  • Riots happen.
  • Act 3 Scene 3
  • Cinna the poet is killed by everyone, because people mistake him for Cinna the conspirator
  • Act 4 Scene 1
  • Anthony, Lepidus and Octavius start planning ways to avenge Caesar's death
  • They made a hit list (list of people to kill)
  • Act 4 Scene 2
  • Cassius accuses Brutus of taking bribes
  • Act 4 Scene 3
  • Cassius and Brutus start to argue over who took the bribe or who didn't
  • Brutus tells Cassius that his wife Portia killed herself
  • They make up and hug it out
  • They put together an army to go and approach Antony and Octavius
  • The ghost of Caesar shows up to Brutus and tells him that he will be revenged
  • Act 5 Scene 1
  • Brutus and Cassius begin to argue with Octavius and Anthony before they begin to fight
  • Act 5 Scene 2
  • Brutus sends someone to tell Cassius he is winning the battle against Octavius
  • Act 5 Scene 3
  • Cassius begins to retreat
  • He gets Titinius to go see if the troops on their way are allies or enemies
  • Titinius gets captured
  • Cassius is upset and has Pindarus stab him with the same dagger that he killed Caesar with
  • Titinius enters with Messala and sees Cassius's body and stabs himself and dies after seeing Cassius dead
  • Brutus comes in and can't believe how powerful Caesar is after his death and he is crying and can't believe it.
  • Act 5 Scene 4
  • Brutus starts to get ready for another battle with the Romans
  • Lucillius pretends to be Brutus, and the Romans capture him and they realize he isn't Brutus
  • Anthony sends his army on a search for the real Brutus
  • Act 5 Scene 5
  • Brutus kills himself
  • He believed it was time for him to die.
  • Anthony and his entourage show up and they find him dead
  • They buried him in an honorable and just way
  • They ended the play by going off and celebrating their victory.
