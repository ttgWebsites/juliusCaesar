Characters

  • Julius Caesar
  • He is a very powerful Roman General and leader
  • He returns to Rome after a victorious campaign, his leadership has made him so powerful that he gains the throne
  • Julius has a major flaw however that proves fatal, he overestimates his safety and does not pay attention to threats, and also he struggles separating public and private life.
  • Brutus
  • Brutus appears to be one of Caesar's most faithful friends.
  • Despite his loyalties to Caesar, he is far more loyal to his homeland.
  • He believes that giving up Caesar is the best for Rome, and in doing so he loses his best friend.
  • Cassius
  • Cassius is another Roman General
  • Cassius becomes very jealous of Caesar's new power, and will not stop until he takes down Caesar
  • He persuades Brutus to kill Caesar by forging various letters calling into question the good that Caesar is doing for Rome
  • Antony
  • Antony proves a loyal friend to Caesar throughout the play.
  • When Caesar dies he gives a speech at the funeral claiming Brutus as a traitor.
  • Octavius
  • Octavius is Caesar's successor, and had been an adopted son of Caesar
  • He is traveling when he hears the news that his father was murdered.
  • He therefore returns to Rome and joins Antony, he wants to avenge his father's death
  • Casca
  • Casca is a very vocal figure set out against Caesar from the beginning
  • He views Caesar as a fraud
  • Cicero
  • He is a powerful speaker and vocal against support of Caesar
  • He later dies on the order of Octavius and Antony
  • Lepidus
  • Lepidus joins forces with Octavius and Antony to help avenge Caesar's death
